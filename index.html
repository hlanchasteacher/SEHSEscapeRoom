🚨 MISSION BRIEFING 🚨
Escape from the Homeostasis Malfunction

The Scenario: You and your team of DP1 Sports Scientists have been locked inside the high-tech SEHS Physiology Lab by a rogue AI system.

The AI has completely destabilized the "Virtual Athlete" in the lab, causing its physiological systems to crash. To unlock the lab doors and escape, you must restore the Virtual Athlete’s homeostasis by solving EIGHT system-based puzzles.

Room 1: The Control Center (Nervous System)

The Virtual Athlete has lost all sensation and autonomic control! The AI presents you with a corrupted circuit board. You must identify the correct sensory receptors.

Match the system alerts to the correct receptor/nerve type to generate the 4-digit override code:

  1. "Alert: Chemical imbalance! Blood pH is dropping due to an increase in CO2"
  2. "Alert: Blood pressure dropping in the aorta!"
  3. "Alert: The athlete is falling! We don't know where our limbs are!"
  4. "Action required: We need to send these signals *towards* the Central Nervous System (CNS)."

Code Options:
1.Efferent | 2.Chemoreceptors | 3.Baroreceptors | 4.Proprioceptors | 5.Afferent

ERROR: Invalid Code. The AI laughs at you.

Room 2: The Transport Hub (Cardiovascular System)

Sensory input is restored, but the Virtual Athlete's blood flow is a mess. The AI demands you calculate the exact blood volume needed to power the athlete's sprint.

Calculate the exact Cardiac Output (Q) in milliliters per minute using Fick's variables:

  • Variable A: The athlete's heart is beating at 150 beats per minute (HR).
  • Variable B: The heart is pumping exactly 70 milliliters (SV) per beat.
ERROR: Incorrect volume. Athlete's systems failing.

Room 3: The Gas Exchange (Respiratory System)

Blood is flowing, but the cells are suffocating! You must optimize the oxygen delivery system.

Combine the numbers from these two questions to form the 3-digit code:

  1. How many molecules of Oxygen (O2) can a single hemoglobin molecule hold? (1 digit)
  2. What percentage of human blood is composed of Plasma? (2 digits)
ERROR: O2 levels critically low. Try again.

Room 4: The Chemical Messengers (Endocrine System)

The athlete's energy and water levels are dangerously fluctuating!

Match the emergency physiological crisis to the exact hormone needed. Use the first letter of each correct hormone to spell the password.

  1. The blood sugar is far too high! We need the hormone that opens cell channels to store glucose as glycogen.
  2. The athlete is rapidly losing water through sweat. The hypothalamus needs to send a hormone to the kidneys to reabsorb water and produce less urine.
  3. The athlete needs an explosive "turbo boost" for a "fight or flight" response! We need this hormone from the adrenal medulla.
ERROR: Incorrect hormone sequence.

Room 5: The Filtration Unit (Water Balance)

The Virtual Athlete is severely dehydrated. The osmoregulation system is failing!

The previous room mentioned a hormone that stops water loss. Now, identify the exact organ that this hormone acts upon to reabsorb water and regulate osmotic pressure.

Spell the name of this organ (plural form) to unlock the door.

ERROR: Osmotic pressure still falling. Organ incorrect.

Room 6: The Fuel Depot (Nutrition & RED-S)

The AI warns of a systems crash due to Low Energy Availability (LEA).

The Virtual Athlete (a biological female) is risking a condition known as Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport (RED-S), which compromises bone health and performance.

Enter the exact optimal Energy Availability (EA) number (in kcal/kg FFM/day) required to maintain healthy physiological functions.

ERROR: Energy Availability too low. Input correct target.

Room 7: The Power Plant (Energy Systems)

The cells are running out of ATP! You must sequence the energy systems based on their ATP production yield.

Type the net ATP yield (per molecule of fuel) of the three systems in order from shortest duration system to longest duration system. Format: X-X-XX.

  • System A: Phosphagen (CrP) system
  • System B: Glycolytic (Lactic Acid) system
  • System C: Aerobic (Oxidative) system
ERROR: Mitochondria reboot failed.

Room 8: The Final Balance (Thermoregulation)

The lab is freezing! The AC has dropped to 0°C. The Virtual Athlete's core body temperature is dropping toward 34°C (Hypothermia).

Which TWO effectors must the Hypothalamus activate to survive the cold? (Enter the two letters, e.g. AC)

  • A. Stimulate sweat glands to evaporate water.
  • B. Cause Vasoconstriction of arterioles to decrease blood flow to the skin.
  • C. Cause Vasodilation of arterioles to increase blood flow to the skin.
  • D. Initiate shivering to produce thermogenesis in skeletal muscles.
ERROR: Homeostasis not achieved. Athlete freezing.

🔓 ESCAPE SUCCESSFUL! 🔓

The Virtual Athlete's core temperature stabilizes at 37°C. The heart rate returns to a resting 60 bpm, energy stores are optimized, and the lab doors slide open.

Congratulations, Sports Scientists! You have survived the Physiology Lab Lockdown!
